Environment Impact

The oil and gas industry is a primary contributor to greenhouse gas emissions, particularly carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming and climate change.


1. Greenhouse Gas Emissions (CO2, Methane)

The oil and gas industry is a primary contributor to greenhouse gas emissions, particularly carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming and climate change.

› Carbon Dioxide (CO2)


The burning of fossil fuels, such as oil and natural gas, releases CO2 into the atmosphere. This is the primary driver of climate change. The oil and gas industry is responsible for approximately 25% of global CO2 emissions.

› Methane (CH4)


Methane is a potent greenhouse gas that is emitted throughout the oil and gas supply chain, from extraction to transportation and storage. Methane leaks from oil and gas wells, pipelines, and storage facilities contribute significantly to global warming. Methane is approximately 25 times more potent as a greenhouse gas than CO2 over a 100-year period.


2. Air Pollutants (Carbon Monoxide, Nitrogen Oxides, Particulate Matter)

The oil and gas industry also releases a variety of air pollutants that can have negative health and environmental consequences. These include:

› Carbon Monoxide (CO)


A colorless, odorless gas that is produced during incomplete combustion of fossil fuels. Exposure to high levels of CO can be fatal.

› Nitrogen Oxides (NO2)


A group of gases that contribute to smog, acid rain, and respiratory problems. NOx emissions from the oil and gas industry can also react with other pollutants to form ozone, a harmful air pollutant that can irritate the lungs and exacerbate respiratory diseases.

› Particulate Matter (PM)


Tiny particles suspended in the air that can cause respiratory problems and contribute to heart disease. PM emissions from the oil and gas industry can come from various sources, including combustion processes, dust from drilling operations, and emissions from oil and gas facilities.


3. Ground and Water Contamination Risks (Spills, Leaks from Storage)

Oil and gas operations can pose risks to groundwater and soil quality due to spills, leaks, and improper disposal of waste.

› Oil Spills


Accidental spills of oil and other petroleum products can contaminate soil and water, harming wildlife and ecosystems. Large-scale oil spills, such as the Deepwater Horizon disaster in the Gulf of Mexico, can have devastating long-term environmental consequences.

› Leaks from Storage


Improperly maintained storage tanks and pipelines can lead to leaks of hazardous substances into the environment. These leaks can contaminate soil and groundwater, posing risks to human health and ecosystems.

› Wastewater Disposal


The disposal of wastewater from oil and gas operations can contaminate groundwater if not managed properly. Wastewater from oil and gas operations may contain a variety of pollutants, including hydrocarbons, salts, and heavy metals.
